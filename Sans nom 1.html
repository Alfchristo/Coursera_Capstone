# Introduction/Business Problem:

The aim of this project is to study the gastronomical similarities or differences between two French cities located in opposite geographic locations, in this case North and South, through the statistical distribution of their restaurant venue categories. The rich cultural heritage of France and proximity to other European states is the reason why this geographic setting and business parameters were chosen. To study possible gastronomic similarities, the project will base itself on all categories of restaurants, a venue capable of expressing cultural heritage and differences in a setting accessible to all in the cities of Lille and Montpellier, Northern and Southern cities respectively.


The cities of Lille and Montpellier were chosen because of their near similarities in terms of population and area size compared to other neighbouring major cities while factoring their administrative limits and not their urban areas. Lille has a population of 233,098 (as of January 2018) for a city limit of 34.8km2 whereas Montpellier has a population of 290,053 (as of January 2018) for a city limit of 56.88km2.


The resulting study may highlight culinary interests in those wishing to travel to these cities or further show geographic influences, if any, that can shape a city. It can also be used for those looking to exploit where a particular culinary venue type may be beneficial in these cities and reduce risks of market saturation.
